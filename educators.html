How the lesson works

Students use a two-axis matrix to place real economic thinkers — not to label them, but to practice a specific set of reasoning skills: identifying assumptions, distinguishing values from facts, acknowledging alternative interpretations, and revising positions in light of evidence.

The lesson has three facilitation options depending on your available time and class structure:

  • Option A — Single period (60 min): Framing, teacher modeling, guided group practice, whole-class share-out.
  • Option B — Extended period (90 min): Adds independent practice and a closing reflection activity.
  • Option C — Two-day sequence: Full lesson plus extension — students place a public figure not studied, or interview a caregiver about their economic worldview.

Assessment guidance

Evaluate students on use of evidence, understanding of the axes, ability to acknowledge alternative placements, and clarity of reasoning. Do not assess based on ideological position. The goal is defensible argument, not correct placement.
